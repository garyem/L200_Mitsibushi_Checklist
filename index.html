Under the Bonnet
0/6
Engine starts cold — no smoke or rattles
Ask to view it cold. Blue smoke = oil burning. White = coolant. Rattle on startup = timing chain.
Oil level correct, no milky residue on cap
Milky oil = coolant mixing with oil = potential head gasket failure
Coolant level correct, no oily residue
Oily coolant = transmission cooler failure — a known L200 issue on auto gearboxes
No oil leaks visible on engine or underneath
Check the ground where the vehicle was parked. Fresh oil stains are a red flag.
Turbo sounds healthy — no whine or lag
Listen for whistle or delay in power delivery. Turbo replacement = £800–£1,500.
No warning lights on dashboard
DPF, engine, ABS and 4WD lights are the key ones to watch for

Test Drive
0/6
📍 Drive Route Tip Insist on at least 20 minutes. Include a B-road for suspension, a dual carriageway for 50–70mph vibration check, and some slow-speed manoeuvres for steering and 4WD.

No vibration at 50–70mph
Propshaft vibration is a known L200 issue. Should feel smooth at motorway speed.
Gearbox shifts smoothly through all gears
Auto should shift without hesitation, clunks or slipping. Test kickdown too.
Steering feels straight — no pull to one side
Pulling suggests tracking or suspension issue. L200 steering can be over-assisted but should still be accurate.
Brakes feel progressive with no grinding
Test at various speeds. Grinding = worn discs/pads. Pulling = seized caliper.
4WD system switches through all modes
Test 2H, 4H and 4L. Warning light should clear within seconds of switching.
Air conditioning blows cold
AC recharge costs £100–£150 if not. A/C faults are a common minor issue on the L200.
